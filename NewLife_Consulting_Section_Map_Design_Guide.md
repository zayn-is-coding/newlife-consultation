## 3. Design Guide

### 3.1 Brand Tone Translation (Bold + Playful + Warm)
- **Bold:** strong type contrast, confident headline sizing, generous color blocking, clear visual hierarchy — avoid timid, low-contrast layouts.
- **Playful:** rounded shapes, friendly micro-interactions (hover states, button bounce), illustrative icon style over generic stock icons.
- **Warm:** soft shadows over harsh lines, rounded corners, human photography (once provided) over abstract graphics, generous white space so the page breathes rather than feels corporate/cold.

### 3.2 Typography
- **Headline font:** a confident, slightly rounded or friendly display typeface (avoid sharp corporate serifs — lean toward something approachable but still credible for a financial-adjacent service).
- **Body font:** clean, highly legible sans-serif, optimized for readability at small sizes (mobile-first).
- **Hierarchy:** maintain consistent scale across pages — H1 (hero only) > H2 (section titles) > H3 (card/sub-section titles) > body > small/caption.
- **Line length:** cap body text around 60–75 characters per line for readability.

### 3.3 Layout & Grid
- 12-column responsive grid, mobile-first breakpoints.
- Section padding: generous vertical rhythm (e.g., 80–120px desktop, 48–64px mobile) to reinforce "bold but breathable."
- Max content width: ~1200–1280px container, full-bleed allowed for hero and CTA bands.
- Cards (services, value props): consistent rounded-corner radius across all card components for visual cohesion.

### 3.4 Components
- **Buttons:** rounded/pill-shaped primary CTA, bold fill color, clear hover/active state, consistent sizing across pages.
- **Icons:** single consistent icon set (line or duotone style) — no mixing icon families.
- **Forms:** large touch-friendly fields, rounded inputs, inline validation, friendly microcopy (not "Submit" — something warmer like "Send My Message").
- **Testimonial/trust blocks:** card-based, with optional avatar circle placeholder until photos are provided.
- **Navigation:** simple horizontal nav (5 items max), sticky on scroll, mobile hamburger with full-screen friendly menu animation.

### 3.5 Imagery Direction
- Until client photos arrive: use warm-toned placeholder imagery or custom illustration in brand style — avoid generic stiff corporate stock photography that conflicts with "playful/warm" tone.
- Once client photos are provided: maintain consistent crop ratio, warm color grading/filter to match palette, rounded image containers to match component style.

### 3.6 Motion & Interaction (light-touch)
- Subtle fade/slide-in on scroll for section reveals.
- Button hover: scale or color-shift, not abrupt.
- Booking/contact form: friendly success-state animation or message after submission.

### 3.7 Accessibility Notes
- Maintain WCAG AA contrast ratios even within the bold/warm palette — verify CTA text against button fill color.
- Ensure all interactive elements (buttons, form fields, nav) have visible focus states.
- Alt text required for all images once content is supplied.
